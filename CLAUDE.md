# The Long Game Project - Website Redesign

## Design Context

### Users
Broad mix of senior leaders, founders, and decision-makers across industries. These are busy, strategic people who have been burned by generic consulting before. They're looking for something genuinely different - not another slide deck factory, but a partner who will challenge their thinking. They arrive sceptical and need to be won over quickly with credibility and distinctiveness.

### Brand Personality
A blend of **Bold + Sharp + Provocative** and **Wise + Calm + Authoritative**. The Long Game Project challenges conventional thinking (provocative, sharp) but does so from a place of deep expertise and credibility (wise, authoritative). Not aggressive - confident. Not loud - resonant.

### Emotional Journey
1. **"This is different"** - Immediate intrigue and curiosity. This isn't the usual consulting pitch.
2. **"These people get it"** - Credibility builds. They understand the real problems with strategy.
3. **"I need to work with them"** - Urgency mixed with confidence. This is what's been missing.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Warm editorial with zine energy** - like a premium strategy journal that borrows confidence from zine/indie publishing culture. Bold typography that commands attention, intentional whitespace, warm tones.
- **Keep the blue** (#0080FF) as the primary brand colour, used as a confident accent on warm/light backgrounds - not as a neon glow on dark mode.
- **Mork Borg-inspired boldness** - not the dark/apocalyptic aesthetic, but the typographic confidence, the willingness to let large type breathe, the mixing of typeface weights and styles, the rejection of corporate polish in favour of authored personality.
- The site should feel like a premium strategy publication with editorial guts - not a SaaS landing page or a corporate consulting brochure.

### Anti-references (what to avoid)
- Generic consulting sites (stock photos, blue gradients, buzzwords)
- Tech startup dark mode + neon brutalism (the current rebuild attempt)
- Academic/text-heavy/boring layouts
- Overly playful with too many animations

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial warmth over tech coldness** - Warm neutrals as the base, blue as a confident accent. Typography-led design. The feel of a beautifully designed strategy journal.
2. **Substance over spectacle** - Every element earns its place. No decorative noise, no glitch effects, no floating shapes. Content and ideas are the hero.
3. **Confident restraint** - Bold statements delivered calmly. Large type balanced with generous whitespace. The confidence comes from what you choose NOT to do.
4. **Distinctively human** - This is a people business. The site should feel authored, opinionated, and personal - not templated or corporate.
5. **Progressive disclosure** - Don't overwhelm. Let visitors discover depth as they explore. Intrigue first, detail on demand.

### Site Structure (Full Site)
- **Home** - Hero, value proposition, problem/solution framing, service overview, social proof, CTA
- **About** - The founders, philosophy, approach, why this exists
- **Services** (individual pages for each):
  - Workshops (tabletop exercises)
  - Masterminds (peer cohorts)
  - Coaching (1:1)
- **Case Studies / Results** - Proof and testimonials
- **Blog / Insights** (future - design the structure, content later)
- **Contact** - Enquiry form, booking link
