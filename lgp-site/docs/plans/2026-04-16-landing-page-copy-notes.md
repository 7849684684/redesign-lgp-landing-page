## Premise

Strategy is messy. Most consulting tools pretend it isn't. They sell tidy
frameworks, neat 2x2s, polished decks — as if the territory ever matched the
map. It doesn't. That's the whole point.

The Long Game Project starts from the opposite premise: strategy is alive.
It moves. It contradicts itself. It needs to be played, paused, rewound,
re-cut. Our job isn't to hand you a tidier map. It's to teach you to read
the territory while it's shifting under your feet.

## The Four Principles (how we work)

Working title: **The Four Principles** / **How we work** / **What we believe**

1. **The map is not the territory.**
   Frameworks are useful right up until you mistake them for the thing
   itself. We use tools lightly, we throw them out faster.

2. **Strategy is a verb.**
   It isn't a document you finish. It's a practice you run. We design for
   ongoing motion, not one-off deliverables.

3. **Sharp questions beat slick answers.**
   The work isn't to look smart in the room. It's to make you smarter after
   we leave it. Provocation is a feature.

4. **Play the long game.**
   Most consulting optimises for the next quarter — and the next invoice.
   We optimise for the decade. The name is the promise.

(Alt principle if we want to swap one out: **"Few clients. Deep work."** —
the scarcity / quality angle.)

## The Four Speeds of Strategy

Concept: strategy isn't one tempo. It runs at four. Visual / interactive
section — each speed gets an icon and a short paragraph. Animation cue:
the four buttons act like a media player. Hover or click to reveal.

- ⏸ **Pause** — Step out of the noise. See the board. Most leaders never
  stop long enough to think. Workshops live here.

- ▶ **Play** — Move. Test. Ship. Strategy that doesn't touch the work is
  theatre. Coaching lives here.

- ⏪ **Rewind** — Go back. What did we miss? What did we learn? The
  un-sexy work of honest review. Masterminds live here.

- ⏩ **Fast forward** — Jump ahead. Pre-mortem the future. Stress-test
  the plan before reality does it for you. Tabletop exercises live here.

## Voice reminders (for whoever takes this to v2)

- Bold + sharp + provocative, but never aggressive.
- Wise + calm + authoritative, but never academic.
- Short sentences. Cut every adverb you can.
- No buzzwords. No "synergies." No "unlock." No "bespoke."
- Say the obvious thing other consultancies won't.
- It's okay to be funny. It's not okay to be cute.
